CX Strategy for British Airways

An in-depth analysis of customer reviews using Natural Language Processing to identify key service pain points and provide actionable recommendations for improving the customer experience.

Project Goal

The primary objective was to move beyond simple star ratings and understand the *why* behind customer sentiment. By applying topic modeling and sentiment analysis, we aimed to pinpoint specific areas—like baggage handling, in-flight service, or booking issues—that were driving negative feedback.

Methodology & Approach

  • Scraped thousands of customer reviews from trusted travel websites.
  • Performed text preprocessing to clean and normalize the review data.
  • Applied Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) for topic modeling to automatically discover recurring themes in the feedback.
  • Used a pre-trained sentiment analysis model to quantify the emotional tone associated with each identified topic.
  • Visualized the findings in a dashboard to clearly present the most impactful areas for improvement.

Impact & Results

The analysis revealed that "unexpected fees at check-in" and "in-flight entertainment system failures" were two of the strongest drivers of negative sentiment, despite not being the most frequently mentioned topics. This insight allowed British Airways to prioritize fixing these high-impact issues, leading to a measurable improvement in customer satisfaction scores in subsequent quarters.
